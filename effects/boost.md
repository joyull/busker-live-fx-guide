# Solo Boost

Lifts volume and adds **mid-range presence** for solo sections. Unlike a generic booster pedal, Mid Focus and Limiter are tuned for acoustic guitar.

![Solo Boost screen](../screenshots/effect-boost.png)

## Layout

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Solo Boost                      [ ON ]     │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│   🎛 Boost   🎛 Saturate                    │
│   🎛 Mid Focus   [ Limiter: ON ]            │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Parameters

| Param | Range | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **Boost** | 0–15 dB | Overall volume boost |
| **Saturate** | 0–100 % | Soft-clip saturation — thickens tone and adds harmonics |
| **Mid Focus** | 0–100 % | 1–3 kHz presence bump — makes the solo cut through |
| **Limiter** | ON / OFF | Peak limiter to protect speakers / the amp |

## Scenarios

### Plain solo boost
- Boost **+6 dB**, Saturate 10 %, Mid Focus 30 %, Limiter ON
- Kick on via footswitch going into a chorus or solo

### Slight drive flavor
- Boost +4 dB, Saturate 40 %, Mid Focus 20 %
- Adds grit/edge to acoustic strumming without going full overdrive

### Presence + body
- Boost +8 dB, Saturate 15 %, Mid Focus 60 %, Limiter ON
- Cuts through a band mix; high Mid Focus secures the presence band

## MIDI Tip

Map a footswitch to **Boost Bypass** — see [MIDI guide](../midi.md) → Effect section → Boost.

## Cautions

- Boost + high AMP Gain can cause **feedback / unwanted distortion**. Keep the Limiter on.
- Saturate above 50 % starts to rob the acoustic of its natural feel. Use 10–30 % for solo emphasis.
- Live, keep **Boost ≤ +6 dB** as a safe ceiling — more than that can throw off the mix balance.
